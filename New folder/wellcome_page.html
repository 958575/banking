BASIC RULES:

1. User Authentication: Before granting access to an online banking account, the bank typically requires users to authenticate themselves. This often involves a combination of a username and a strong password. Some banks may also use two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security, such as sending a one-time code to a registered mobile device.
2.Account Access: Once authenticated, users can access their account information, including checking balances, reviewing transactions, and accessing electronic statements. They may also be able to manage and transfer funds between accounts.
3.Transaction History: Online banking allows users to view a detailed history of their transactions. This includes records of deposits, withdrawals, transfers, and payments made from the account.
